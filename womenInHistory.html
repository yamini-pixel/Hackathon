Rosalind Franklin

During the late 1940s to 50s, Franklin discovered DNA density and established the helical conformation of DNA. She discovered a second type of DNA, B-DNA, the structure that DNA changes into when exposed to high levels of moisture. Through her X-ray diffraction and DNA work, Franklin was able make clearer X-ray pattern, such as Photo 51, of DNA molecules.

Valentina Tereshkova

In 1963, at the age of 26, Tereshkova became the first and youngest woman in space. After being sellected from a pool of over 400 applicants, she was launched into space on the Vostok 6 mission. To this day she is the only woman who had flown solo into space.

Betty Friedan

Friedan was one of the early leader of the women's right movement in the United States in the 1960s and 1970s. In 1963, Friedan published "The Feminine Mystique" which sparked the second wave of American feminism in the 20th century. This book highlighted limited gender role frustrations of many American women and inspired public activism for gender equality.

Serena Williams

Williams is considered one of the greatest and successful tennis player. She won Grand Slam singles titles more than any other person. She is also know for her powerful play and athletic ablitlies. Her tennis journey started in 1991 when she started attending a tennis academy. Then four years later she turned professional and a few years after that she won her first Grand Slam singles title. For the many years after that, she went on to win 22 more Grand Slam singles titles.

Taylor Swift

Swift is an American singer-songwriter known for her narrative songwriting that spans various genres. She started her career in 2014, at the age of 14. Since then, Swift was nomiated for the Grammy for 42 times and won 11 times. In addition to her influences in the music industry, she also more generally influences the world by donating and launching camgaigns to fight problems related to education, LGBTQIA+, internet sex crimes, hunger and etc.
